My Journey started from...


I am originally from South Korea, but I have travelled to several places and have been living in Melbourne, Australia for 8 years.

I came to Australia with one backpack and a two-month travelling plan with the intention of learning more about fitness instructing, as I was already a fitness instructor in Korea. But quickly, I fell so in love with this city that I never went back home.

I have previously travelled across 11 different states in America on a 6-week road trip and have also visited Sydney, Brisbane and the Sunshine coast in Queensland.

I have also travelled to Fukuoka in Japan on a cruise ship from Korea. Which was a lot of fun! Gangnam style was hitting the world back then so I did a lot of that “horse dancing” with Japanese strangers haha! I have also travelled to Baja in Mexico on a motorcycle! It was rough to be on a motorcycle for a long time but was a lifetime experience!

My career in the past...


I have always LOVED dancing since I started being able to walk. I used to teach K-pop dance choreography back in Korea and that interest developed further into fitness instructing. In fact, I have been instructing various classes at many different fitness facilities back home and here in Melbourne city for 14 years now.

The combination of my passion for the movement of the human body through exercise and my interest in clinical knowledge drove me to go back to university in 2018 to study a bachelor of Health Science, majoring in human anatomy and physiology.

Upon the completion of my degree, I was offered a full time job at a Haematology lab where I now help perform the full blood tests and much more! (Pretty much whatever your doctor requests!)

How I got into coding...


As the pandemic hit in 2020, just like many of you, I was stuck at home finishing my bachelor degree. I was also teaching dance fitness classes via Zoom to Koreans and my gym clients here. Some requested if I can pre-record the classes and make it available to them. So I attempted to make videos with the intention of using Youtube but this later developed into an idea of “how can I make my own platform to put all my contents where I can also make revenue?”

I started teaching myself HTML, CSS through various online materials. This continued even after getting a full time job. I also started seeing everyday problems around me. For example, the Sonic Health software made our lives a lot easier with automation of test. However, it had many flaws which I had to learn to get around in my daily work. An example would be when certain tests had been done but this would only be recognised after the specimen has been stored twice. Which made the work inefficient.

Seeing these problems made my learning more engaging and personal. Leaving full time work in the middle of pandemic was one of the hardest decisions but I took the risk as I was too curious and really wanted to learn programming seriously.
